# Field Scoring Cheat Sheet (1-5)

Use this during observations to keep scoring consistent across runs.

## Quick interpretation

- **Low (good for artifact scores):** `1-2`
- **Moderate:** `3`
- **High (bad for artifact scores):** `4-5`
- **Detail score is reversed:** high detail (`4-5`) is good.

## Halo score (lower is better)

1. Very low: faint thin glow near object edge.
2. Low: compact halo, limited local impact.
3. Medium: obvious halo, some contrast loss.
4. High: broad halo, nearby sky visibly washed.
5. Very high: dominant flare-like glow.

## Veiling score (lower is better)

1. Dark background preserved.
2. Slight background lift near bright areas.
3. Moderate haze over local region.
4. Strong veil across broad region.
5. Heavy veil; fine contrast mostly buried.

## Ghost brightness score (lower is better)

1. Barely detectable ghost.
2. Faint repeatable ghost.
3. Clear ghost, not dominant.
4. Bright ghost competes with detail.
5. Very bright ghost strongly disruptive.

## Detail score (higher is better)

1. Very low: only coarse shape.
2. Low: coarse detail, little fine structure.
3. Medium: moderate fine detail.
4. High: good fine detail, stable edges.
5. Very high: crisp detail near seeing/tracking limits.

## Practical note

Keep target, eyepiece, and focus method as constant as possible.  
Only change one test variable at a time (aperture *or* baffle state).
